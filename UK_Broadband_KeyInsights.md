 Key Insights from the UK Broadband Dashboard
 1. Urban areas significantly outperform rural areas
Avg Urban Speed: 87.38 Mbps

Avg Rural Speed: 28.81 Mbps  
This shows a 3× performance gap, highlighting a persistent digital divide between rural and urban communities.

 2. Rural coverage is strong but not matched by speed
Rural Coverage: 97.18%  
Even though coverage is high, rural speeds remain low, suggesting that availability does not equal performance.

 3. Virgin Media emerges as the top-performing provider
Highest download speeds

Strong value score (Speed per £ = 2.64)

Consistently outperforms BT, Sky, Vodafone, TalkTalk, and Plusnet
Virgin Media leads both in speed and cost efficiency.

 4. Broadband costs vary widely across providers
Avg monthly cost ranges from £20–£40

Virgin Media and BT sit at the higher end

Plusnet and TalkTalk remain the most budget‑friendly
This indicates a clear trade‑off between cost and performance.

 5. East Midlands stands out as the top UK region
Strong download speeds

Competitive monthly costs

Balanced rural and urban performance
This region offers the best overall broadband value.

 6. Upload speeds remain low across all providers
Avg Upload Speed: 7.57 Mbps  
Despite improvements in download speeds, upload performance lags behind — a challenge for remote work, cloud use, and content creators.

 7. Speed per £ reveals major value differences
Virgin Media delivers the best value

Some providers charge more without offering proportional speed gains
This metric highlights inefficiencies in pricing vs. performance.

 8. Regional cost differences do not always align with speed
Some high‑cost regions do not deliver higher speeds

Indicates potential market imbalance or limited competition in certain areas
