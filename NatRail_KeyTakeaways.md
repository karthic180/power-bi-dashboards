# **Overview**

This project provides an analytical breakdown of over **500,000 UK National Rail ticket transactions**, focusing on customer purchasing behaviour, journey performance, revenue trends, refund patterns, and station‑level travel insights. The dataset is modelled and visualised in Power BI to support operational decision‑making, performance monitoring, and strategic planning across the rail network.

The analysis covers six core areas: **Purchases and Transactions**, **Journey Analysis**, **Ticket Type and Class**, **Refund Requests**, **Station and Destination Trends**, and **Ticket Price Analysis**.

---

# **Key Insights Summary**

## **1. Transactions and Purchase Behaviour**
- More than **500K transactions** recorded across the network.  
- **Online purchases (58%)** exceed station purchases.  
- **Credit card** is the most frequently used payment method.  
- Railcard usage is dominated by **Adult Railcards (66%)**.

## **2. Revenue and Pricing**
- Total revenue exceeds **£30 million**.  
- **Return tickets generate 40%** of all revenue.  
- **Average ticket price: £23.44**.  
- **First Class average price: £75**, significantly higher than Standard Class.  
- Revenue by ticket type ranks: **Anytime**, **Off‑Peak**, **Advance**.

## **3. Journey Performance**
- Majority of journeys operate **on time**, though delays remain significant.  
- **Signal failures** are the leading cause of delays, followed by staffing issues.  
- Total delay duration exceeds **97,000 minutes**.  
- Delayed journeys strongly correlate with refund requests.

## **4. Refund Requests**
- Only **3%** of all transactions result in a refund.  
- Refunds are primarily due to delays exceeding **30 minutes**.  
- Standard Class accounts for most refunds due to higher passenger volume.

## **5. Station and Destination Trends**
- **London King’s Cross** is the busiest departure station with over **50,000 journeys**.  
- Other major hubs include **London Euston**, **Birmingham New Street**, **Manchester Piccadilly**, and **Edinburgh Waverley**.  
- Arrival patterns mirror the busiest departure stations, indicating strong inter‑city demand.

## **6. Ticket Type and Class Trends**
- **Standard Class** represents **90%** of all journeys.  
- First Class contributes disproportionately to revenue despite lower volume.  
- Advance tickets are widely used but generate less revenue due to lower pricing.
